Workshop Overview

Wildfires are increasingly shaped by compound drivers such as climate variability, fuel dynamics, land-use change, and rapid expansion of the Wildland-Urban Interface. At the same time, advances in AI and large-scale spatial-temporal data have created new opportunities to improve wildfire prediction, operational infrastructure, and decision support. This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners across AI, remote sensing, wildfire science, and emergency management to build a shared agenda for next-generation wildfire science and technology. We highlight pathways that connect predictive modeling with actionable systems, including real-time data integration, scalable forecasting and uncertainty quantification, risk-aware infrastructure planning, and stakeholder-centered decision-making. With invited talks, panels, and student presentations, the workshop aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration that translates scientific innovation into operational impact for North Florida and beyond.

Invited Speakers

Ilkay Altintas
Dr. Altintas is the Chief Data Science Officer of the San Diego Supercomputer Center (SDSC) and the Founding Director of the Societal Computing and Innovation Lab. Since joining SDSC in 2001, she has been a principal investigator and a technical leader in a wide range of cross-disciplinary projects. She holds a PhD degree from the University of Amsterdam.
Xilei Zhao
Dr. Zhao is an associate professor in Transportation Engineering at the University of Florida. She received Early-Career Research Fellowship of NASEM's Gulf Research Program. Her research has been supported by multiple funding agencies such as the U.S. Department of Transportation, the National Institute of Standards and Technology, and the U.S. Geological Survey.
Neda Yaghoobian
Dr. Yaghoobian is an Associate Professor of Mechanical Engineering at Florida State University and an Associate of the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Institute. As NSF CAREER awardee, her research focuses on fire dynamics, land-atmosphere interactions, environmental fluid dynamics, turbulence, and computational modeling.
